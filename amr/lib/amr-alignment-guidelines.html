AMR-English Gold Alignment Guidelines      
  Ulf Hermjakob     version as of Nov. 23, 2015

Sections: What to Align   -   What Not to Align   -   Decomposed Words and Multi Word Expressions   -   Names   -   Anaphora   -   Parentheticals   -   Miscellaneous

What to Align

  • Content words (e.g. "eat", "large", "donut")
  • Names (e.g. "The Hague", "Stratford-upon-Avon") with all parts of a name, even articles and punctuation
  • Pronouns (e.g. "I", "your", "himself", "ours", "that", "who")
  • Prepositions (e.g. "of", "for", "instead of")
  • Conjunctions (e.g. "and", "or", "because", "if", "that")
  • Possessive particles (e.g. "'s")
  • Copula (e.g. "is" as in The girl is happy.)
  • Modals (e.g. "may" as in The children may leave.)
  • Support verbs (e.g. "have" as in to have a meeting, "take" as in to take a bath)
  • Verb particles (e.g. "in" as in to give in, "off" as in to take off)
Examples:

(d / destroy-01 :location ... Rome ...) the destruction in Rome
(d / destroy-01 :ARG0 ... Rome ...) the destruction by Rome
(d / destroy-01 :ARG1 ... Rome ...) the destruction of Rome
(c / car :poss ... John ...) John 's car
(a / and :op1 ... John ... :op2 ... Mary ...) John and Mary
:condition (r / rain-01) if it rains
(s / say-01 :ARG0 (h / he) :ARG1 (r / rain-01)) He said that it will rain .
(f / find-01 :ARG0 (g / girl) :ARG1 (a / amr-unknown)) What did the girl find ?
(h / happy :domain (g / girl)) The girl is happy.
(s / sad-02 :ARG1 (g / girl)) The girl is sad.
(p / permit-01 :ARG1 (l / leave-11 :ARG0 (c / child))) The children may leave.
(b / bathe-01 :ARG0 (h / he)) He took a bath .
(t / take-off-19 :ARG1 (p / plane)) The plane took off .

Exceptions

  • Do not co-align the infinitival "to" with the verb that follows.
    But it's ok to align it to :purpose etc.

Examples:

(w / want-01 :ARG0 (d / dolphin) :ARG1 (b / breathe :ARG0 d)) The dolphin wanted to breathe .
(s / surface-01 :ARG1 (d / dolphin) :purpose (b / breathe-01 :ARG0 d)) The dolphin surfaced to breathe .

What Not to Align

  • Articles (e.g. "a", "the")
  • Auxiliary verbs (e.g. "to be", "to have", "to do", "will") as used in the contruction of perfect, pluperfect and future tenses, passives, and continuous forms
  • Dummy pronouns, also called pleonastic pronouns (such as the it in It rains.)
  • Relative pronouns (e.g. "who", "that")
  • Existential there is
  • Punctuation
Examples:

(r / rain-01) It is raining .
(e / explode-01) There was an explosion .

Exceptions

  • Align tokens that are part of a name under :name (n / name :opx ...).
  • Align question marks with any :mode interrogative (in yes/no questions).
  • Align any punctuation that stands for a word (e.g. 20-30 apples = 20 to 30 apples).
  • Align any indefinite article ("a"/"an") to the corresponding "1" in any ":quant 1" annotation.

Examples:

(c / city :wiki "The_Hague" :name (n / name :op1 "The" :op2 "Hague")) The Hague
(s / smoke-02 :ARG0 (y / you) :mode interrogative) You smoke ?
(a / apple :quant (b / between :op1 20 :op2 30)) 20 - 30 apples
(b / before :op1 (n / now) :quant (t / temporal-quantity :quant 1 :unit (y / year))) a year ago

Decomposed Words and Multi Word Expressions

For words that are decomposed in AMR (e.g. teacher = person who teaches), align with all components.
Similarly, align all parts of a multi-word expression.

Examples:

(p / person :ARG0-of (t / teach-01)) teacher
(t / thing :ARG1-of (s / suggest-01)) suggestion
(p / polite :polarity -) impolite
(p / planet :ord (o / ordinal-entity :value 2)) the second planet
(m / more-than :op1 10) more than ten
(c / come-01 :ARG1 (h / he) :concession (r / rain-01)) He came in spite of the rain .

Names

Align names low (i.e. do not align with entity type such as "country" or name).
Do not align a name to :wiki or the wikification value that follows :wiki.
Align all parts of a name, even articles and punctuation.

Examples:

(c / country :wiki "United_States" :name (n / name :op1 "US")) US
(c / country :wiki "North_Korea" :name (n / name :op1 "North" :op2 "Korea")) North Korea
(c / city :wiki "The_Hague" :name (n / name :op1 "The" :op2 "Hague")) The Hague
(c / city :wiki "Stratford-upon-Avon" :name (n / name :op1 "Stratford-upon-Avon")) Stratford @-@ upon @-@ Avon

Anaphora

When an entity plays multiple roles, align any non-anchor anaphora with their respective reentrancy variables.

Examples:

(l / like-01 :ARG0 (b / boy) :ARG1 (d / dog :poss b)) The boy likes his dog .
(a / and :op1 (d / doctor) :op2 (p / person :ARG0-of (h / have-rel-role-91 :ARG1 d :ARG2 (w / wife)))) The doctor and the doctor 's wife .
(l / like-01 :ARG0 (i / i) :ARG1 (a / and :op1 (c / country :wiki "China" :name (n / name "China")) :op2 (f / food :mod c))) I like China and Chinese food .

Parentheticals

Do not align semantically redundant parenthetical expressions such as abbreviations and quantity/entity conversions.

Examples:

(c / country "New_Zealand" :name (n / name :op1 "New" :op2 "Zealand")) New Zealand (NZ)
(d / distance-quantity :quant 80 :unit (k / kilometer)) 80 km (50 miles)

Miscellaneous

  • Align a possessive pronoun (e.g. "my", "their") with any role :poss (or other role that 's would also be aligned to).
  • Align words with a comparative or superlative suffix (-er -est) with any :degree (m / more) etc.
  • Align negation words (e.g. "no", "not") with both :polarity and -.
  • Align :manner with any corresponding adverb (often with -ly ending) or preposition (such as by).
  • Align words with typos, e.g. align undrestood with understand-01.
  • Align both if and then to any corresponding :condition.
  • Align both even and if to both :concession and even-if.
  • Align a month's name to both :month and its number value, but years and days only to their number values.

Examples:

(c / cat :poss (i / i)) my cat
(s / smart :degree (m / more)) smarter
(i / intelligent :degree (m / more)) more intelligent
(r / rain-01 :polarity -) It did not rain.
(s / sing-01 :ARG0 (b / boy) :manner (b2 / beautiful)) The boy sang beautifully .
(s / survive-01 :ARG0 (h / he) :manner (f / fish-01 :ARG0 h)) He survived by fishing .
(d / date-entity :year 2015 :month 8 :day 31) August 31, 2015
